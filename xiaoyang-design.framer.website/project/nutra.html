Problem Identify

"The budgeting applications available in the market seem too complex and bulky for users struggling with ADHD or lacking in self-management experience."

Which Groups Are Being Overlooked？


We reviewed feedback on popular financial management apps to understand the characteristics of different user groups. Our goal was to identify which demographics are not adequately addressed by the current budget management apps available in the market.

How do we recruit interviewees from this population?


We first distribute questionnaires assessing self-control and motivation. Based on the scores, we identify individuals with both "low self-control" and "low motivation" for interviews.



Gaining deeper understanding of this overlooked population.


“Maybe low motivation and low self-control means they don’t need financial management at all?”

  • Goal 1: actual needs for financial management

  • Goal 2: and if they do have needs, the challenges they have encountering self-displinary for financial management

Surprisingly, despite their "low motivation," all eight interviewees expressed a clear need for financial management.

However, they all hesitate to start or end up giving up on managing their finances for various reasons.

Based on the interviews, we identified and summarized three common mental models among the interviewees that hinder their financial management efforts.

Incorporating these shared mindsets - a user journey map for this population to identify the stages where they face the greatest challenges.
